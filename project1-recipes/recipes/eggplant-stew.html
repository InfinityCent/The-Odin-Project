Eggplant Stew

Description

The Eggplant Stew is a key Persian dish enjoyed by anyone living in Iran or abroad. It is tasty, highly customizable, and very simple to make whether you are an experienced chef or just a beginner. This typically consists of fried eggplants and onions mixed with tomato sauce and served over white basmati rice. Seasoning and any additional ingredients are left up to the cook.

Here, we wlil cook white basmati rice, and an eggplant stew with diced chicken, tomatoes, and fajita seasoning.

Ingredients (4 portions)

  • 3 cups of white basmati rice
  • 8 medium eggplants
  • 2 large tomatoes
  • 1 large onion
  • 3 chicken breasts
  • tomato paste
  • canola oil
  • salt
  • pepper
  • fajita seasoning

Cooking Instructions

  1. Mix 6 teaspoons of salt with 3 cups of white basmati rice and soak in water for at least 2 hours.
  2. Slice 8 eggplants in half length-wise and fry them over canola oil. Flip them over and wait until they have become soft. Remove them and store them in a separate container.
  3. Chop 1 large onion and fry over some canola oil until they have turned golden. Do this in the same pan used for frying eggplants.
  4. Dice 3 chicken breasts and add them to the onion, cook until they have turned white.
  5. Boil water and add to the chicken until they are partially submerged.
  6. Add 2 tablespoons of tomato paste to the chicken, mix until it has spread evenly.
  7. Add 2 tablespoons of fajita seasoning to the chicken (or more if desired), mix.
  8. Add salt and pepper, adjust based on preference.
  9. Put chicken on low simmer until the sauce is slightly viscous.
  10. Add fried eggplants to the chicken along with any other ingredients of your choice.
  11. Get a separate pot and fill it with water, put it to boil.
  12. Pour the rice into a sieve and briefly rinse it, then add boiling water. Wait about 10 minutes until the rice has softened (but do not wait so long that it has gone fully soft).
  13. Pour out the boiling water and rice into a sieve again, then add a significant amount of oil to the bottom of the pot used for rice (this will make the cooked rice crispy and delicious - called tahdig).
  14. Put the rice back in the pot and add one cup of water along the of the pot. Put on the lid (wrapped in a towel first) and cook on low heat for 25-30 minutes.
  15. Serve the eggplant stew with rice and enjoy!

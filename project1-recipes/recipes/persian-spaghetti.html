Persian Spaghetti

Description

Everyone is familiar with spaghetti, but the Persian version has several differences that makes it stand out. First the spaghetti itself is on the thicker, shorter side. It it also mixed with tomato paste which turns it slightly orange and provides added flavour. It is also mixed with ground beef and oregano. The highlight is spaghetti tahdig which is the slightly burnt, crispy spaghetti flattened at the bottom of the pot after cooking.

Ingredients (4 portions)

  • 1 pack spaghetti
  • 300 grams ground beef
  • 1 onion
  • turmeric powder
  • salt
  • pepper
  • oregano
  • tomato paste
  • canola oil

Cooking Instructions

  1. Fill a pot with water, add one spoon of salt, and put to boil.
  2. Break spaghetti in half and add to boiling water, wait until soft. Put aside.
  3. Chop 1 large onion and fry over some canola oil in a separate pan until they have turned golden.
  4. Add raw ground beef to the onion and using a spatula, work on separating it into smaller chunks.
  5. When the beef has turned slightly brown, add 1 teaspoon of turmeric powder, salt, pepper, and oregano. Mix.
  6. Add two table spoons of tomato paste to the beef and mix.
  7. Continue cooking beef until it has fully turned brown. Set aside.
  8. In a pot, pour canola oil, then add spaghetti and beef on top. Mix the ingredients.
  9. Add one cup of water along the edges of the pot and cook at low heat for 25-30 minutes.
  10. As a bonus, carefully flip the pot over a large plate so the tahdig is on top. Enjoy!
